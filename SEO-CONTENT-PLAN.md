# Garden & Art Landscapes 90-Day SEO Growth Plan

Prepared: July 16, 2026
Website: `https://www.landscapingraleigh.com/`
Primary market: Raleigh and the Triangle, North Carolina
Primary goal: qualified residential landscape-design and installation inquiries

## Executive direction

Build a measurable local-search system in the first 90 days, then use the evidence it produces to choose the next quarter. Build authority in this order:

1. Make the homepage the definitive page for `landscaping raleigh nc`.
2. Publish dedicated commercial pages for landscape design, drainage, hardscaping, native gardens and outdoor lighting.
3. Support those pages with Raleigh-specific homeowner guides and detailed project stories.
4. Add secondary service and city pages only when there is enough real work, photography and customer proof to make each page genuinely useful.

Garden & Art should compete as a horticulture-led residential design/build specialist, not as a generic mowing company. Norman Rabins's horticultural training, botanical-garden experience, sustainable-water expertise and artistic point of view are the defensible content advantage.

## Research basis and interpretation

This plan combines:

- the current Astro website, sitemap, service inventory and portfolio;
- the July 14 technical/content audit and existing keyword clusters;
- live Keywords Everywhere U.S. clickstream + Google Keyword Planner data collected July 16, 2026;
- live search-result and competitor review for Raleigh landscape design, drainage, hardscaping and native-garden queries;
- current Raleigh homeowner questions and City of Raleigh resources.

Keywords Everywhere reported only two ranking keywords for `landscapingraleigh.com`, both irrelevant and below position 80, with estimated monthly organic traffic of zero. Treat this as an early-site baseline, not a precise analytics measurement.

Important data notes:

- Volume is approximate monthly U.S. search volume for the exact phrase. A locally modified phrase such as `landscape design raleigh nc` is still useful market evidence; a non-local `near me` phrase is national and should not be treated as Raleigh-only demand.
- The `Competition` value is Google Ads advertiser competition from 0 to 1. It is not organic keyword difficulty.
- Zero-volume phrases are not necessarily zero-demand. Keyword tools frequently suppress low-volume local and long-tail queries. Use them when the intent and business fit are strong, but do not build thin standalone pages for every variation.
- CPC is directional commercial-intent evidence, not a revenue forecast.

## 90-day production roadmap

The production target is eight anchor website pages plus at least two search-led posts each week, or 32+ useful indexable releases in three months. Within that larger cadence, the 16 cornerstone assets are five commercial service pages, six priority guides, three detailed project stories, an expert profile and a project hub. This is achievable only when owner interviews, project facts, photography and approvals arrive on schedule. Research and drafting can move quickly; evidence remains the publishing gate.

## Content quality requirements

Every commercial service page should include:

- 800–1,200 useful words, adjusted for the actual subject rather than padded to a quota;
- a clear Raleigh service statement near the top;
- original project photography with descriptive captions;
- the homeowner problem, the approach and the result—not just a gallery;
- process, service boundaries, timeline and price factors;
- two or more proof elements: credential, license, testimonial, project detail or original observation;
- five to eight specific FAQs based on real inquiry questions;
- one primary CTA repeated at natural decision points;
- Service schema plus breadcrumb markup where appropriate.

Every article should include:

- a direct answer in the opening paragraph;
- original Raleigh/NC expertise rather than a generic national rewrite;
- named sources for program, horticultural or regulatory claims;
- a short “when to call a professional” section;
- links to one primary service page, one related guide and one project when available;
- Article and BreadcrumbList schema;
- author attribution to Norman Rabins with relevant credentials.

Every case study should include:

- city or neighborhood-level location with the customer's permission;
- initial conditions and constraints;
- goals, design decisions, materials and plants;
- drainage or maintenance considerations;
- before, process and after photographs;
- measurable or observable result;
- homeowner quotation when permission is available;
- links to every relevant service, but one primary CTA.

## On-page template

For each planned page:

1. Use the primary phrase naturally in the title, H1, first paragraph and URL.
2. Write a title of roughly 50–60 characters and a distinct description of roughly 140–160 characters, prioritizing readability over hard limits.
3. Answer the core intent before company background.
4. Use related phrases in meaningful subsections; do not repeat a keyword mechanically.
5. Include a concise definition or answer block that can be quoted by search engines and AI systems.
6. Pair claims with firsthand detail, photos, named credentials or authoritative local sources.
7. Include one clear canonical URL and ensure the page is in the XML sitemap.

- [GM Stonework & Landscape](https://gmstonework.com/) presents hardscape types, recent projects, process and drainage-aware construction clearly. Garden & Art needs equally concrete project detail while owning the garden-plus-structure angle.

These competitor metrics are directional: Keywords Everywhere reported low estimated traffic and few top-30 ranking keywords for all sampled local domains. That does not measure Google Maps visibility, referrals or total business strength.

## Review cadence and decision rules

- After 30 days: confirm indexing, sitemap inclusion and internal links.
- After 60–90 days: improve titles/openings for pages earning impressions but few clicks; expand sections only when queries reveal missing intent.
- After 90–180 days: split a subservice into its own page only when the parent page ranks for a meaningful distinct query set and the company has enough proof.
- Merge, redirect or reframe pages with overlapping intent rather than allowing cannibalization.
- Refresh costs, programs, plant recommendations and regulations at least annually.
- Re-run Keywords Everywhere and compare domain ranking keywords every quarter, using Search Console as the primary source of truth for this site.
