### What I will handle

**1. Google Business Profile** — Create or claim the listing seen in Google Search and Maps. Complete the services, contact details and photos, then publish up to two updates each month. **Value: Be easier to find and contact locally.**

**2. Google Search Console** — Set up Google’s free search-reporting tool, submit the website, fix indexing problems and see which searches lead people to Garden & Art. **Value: Know how customers find the website.**

**3. Google Analytics** — Set up private website measurement and track contact forms, phone taps and email clicks—not personal customer information. **Value: Connect the work to real inquiries.**

**4. Local website content** — Improve local search details and publish one Raleigh-area project story or useful guide each month within the available time. **Value: Build visibility and explain the work clearly.**

**5. Reviews and reputation** — Create a direct Google review link and ready-to-send request, monitor new reviews and help write thoughtful responses. **Value: Turn happy clients into trusted proof.**

**6. Project photography** — Photograph one focused project each month, including matched before-and-after views, then edit the images for every channel. **Value: Show the quality and transformation visually.**

**7. Instagram and Facebook** — Reuse the strongest project photos, Google updates and website stories on social instead of creating three separate campaigns. **Value: Stay visible in more places without wasting time.**

**8. Mailing list and email** — Set up an opt-in list, branded template and website signup. Send one seasonal email and prepare a quarterly rhythm. **Value: Encourage repeat work and referrals.**

**9. Clear reporting** — Build a live dashboard, send a short weekly update and provide monthly insights on visibility, reviews, calls and inquiries. **Value: See what was done, what worked and what is next.**

### How the 90 days work

**Month 1 — Set up:** Google, tracking, reviews, email and the first photo session.  
**Months 2–3 — Maintain and improve:** photography, Google updates, website content, reviews, reporting and the next highest-value priorities.
