# Raleigh Landscaping Keyword Research

Prepared: July 14, 2026  
Business: Garden & Art Landscapes  
Market: Raleigh and the Triangle, North Carolina

## Executive recommendation

Garden & Art should not compete as a generic lawn-mowing company. The strongest search position is a **horticulture-led residential landscape design and installation company** with uncommon credibility in natural gardens, drainage and rain gardens, hardscapes, irrigation and artistic details.

The initial SEO program should target one broad local homepage cluster and four focused service clusters. Supporting articles and before-and-after project stories should answer Raleigh-specific questions and lead readers back to those service pages.

## Research limitations

The paid keyword-volume provider is not connected, and Search Console has not yet collected data for the redesigned site. Search demand figures are therefore not fabricated in this report. Priorities are based on current Raleigh search results, recurring language used by ranking competitors, local homeowner discussions, existing services and commercial intent. Validate exact monthly volume and current positions in Google Keyword Planner and Search Console after those accounts are connected.

## Before-and-after case-study formula

Project pages can rank for long-tail local searches while providing the proof that service pages need.

Use titles such as:

- North Raleigh Woodland Garden Renovation: Before and After
- Raleigh Backyard Drainage and Rain Garden Transformation
- Cary Flagstone Patio and Native Planting Design
- Raleigh Retaining Wall and Terraced Garden Renovation
- Chapel Hill Shade Garden and Low-Maintenance Planting Plan

Every case study should include the location at city or neighborhood level, the homeowner’s problem, constraints, Norman’s approach, materials and plants, before-and-after photography, results and a link to the relevant service page. Do not publish a location or customer address without permission.

## Measurement plan

Track each keyword cluster as a group rather than celebrating a single ranking.

- Search Console impressions, clicks and average position by target page
- Calls, email clicks and completed inquiry forms from organic traffic
- Google Business Profile calls, website visits and direction requests
- Queries containing Raleigh plus a service term
- Queries mentioning a problem: drainage, clay soil, shade, native plants or low maintenance
- Leads and quoted revenue attributed to each landing page

Revisit this plan after 60–90 days of Search Console data. Keep pages that attract qualified inquiries, improve pages earning impressions but few clicks, and avoid expanding low-value services simply because their keywords appear popular.
